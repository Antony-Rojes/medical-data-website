Severe chest pain or pressure may be a heart attack. The person may look pale, sweaty, or short of breath. Take it seriously and act fast.

Call emergency medical services immediately. Stay calm, explain that the person has chest pain, and follow the operator’s instructions. Do not leave the person alone.

Help the person sit upright comfortably and support their back. Keep them calm and breathing slowly. Do not let them move their arms or legs. Wait for medical help to arrive.

Loosen any tight clothing around the person’s neck, chest, or waist to make breathing easier. Keep them sitting upright and calm. Do not let them move their arms or legs. Stay with them and wait for emergency help.

(Optional) If available, aspirin may be given. First make sure the person is not allergic to aspirin, is conscious, and is able to swallow safely. Give only if these conditions are met and emergency help is on the way.

Keep the person calm and seated in a comfortable upright position. Stay with them, watch their breathing, and do not let them walk or move around. Wait for the ambulance to arrive.
