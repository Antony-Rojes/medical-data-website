Cool the burn immediately under cool running water for 20 minutes. This helps stop the burning process and reduces pain and swelling. Do not use ice.

Remove rings, watches, belts, or tight clothing near the burned area before swelling begins. Do not remove clothing stuck to the burn.

Cover the burn loosely with a clean, non-stick cloth or sterile dressing. This protects the area from infection and reduces pain.

Seek medical help immediately if the burn is deep, blistered, electrical, chemical, or larger than a palm. Call emergency services for severe burns.
