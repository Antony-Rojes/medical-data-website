Apply firm direct pressure to the bleeding wound using a clean cloth or gauze. Hold pressure continuously for 5 to 10 minutes without checking. Most bleeding stops with steady pressure.

Elevate the injured limb above heart level if possible. Use pillows or other support to keep it raised. Elevation helps reduce bleeding and swelling.

Apply an ice pack or cold compress wrapped in a thin cloth. Use for 10 to 15 minutes every hour. Never apply ice directly to the skin.

Once bleeding has stopped, gently clean the wound with clean water or mild antiseptic. Rinse from the center outward and avoid scrubbing the area.

Apply a thin layer of antibiotic ointment to the cleaned wound. This helps prevent infection and supports proper healing.

Cover the wound with sterile gauze and secure it with a bandage. Wrap snugly but not too tight. Check circulation to ensure fingers or toes do not turn blue.
