Kneel beside the person’s chest. Place one hand in the center of the chest, put your other hand on top, and keep your arms straight.

Push your hands straight down on the center of the chest. Use your body weight and press hard and fast.

Allow the chest to fully return after each compression. Then press down again for the next compression.

Tilt the person’s head back and lift the chin to open the airway.

If the person does not respond, begin mouth-to-mouth rescue breathing.

Pinch the person’s nose closed to prevent air from escaping.

Seal your mouth over the person’s mouth and give a slow, steady breath.

Continue alternating chest compressions and rescue breaths until the person starts breathing or emergency help arrives.
