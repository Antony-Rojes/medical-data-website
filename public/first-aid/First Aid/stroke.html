Ask the person to smile. Observe whether one side of the face droops or appears uneven. Facial drooping is an early warning sign of stroke.

Ask the person to raise both arms. Watch closely to see if one arm drifts downward or cannot be lifted fully.

Ask the person to repeat a simple sentence such as “The sky is blue.” Listen for slurred speech, incorrect words, or inability to speak.

Ask if the person is experiencing a sudden, severe headache. A headache described as the worst of their life can indicate a stroke.

Check for sudden nausea or vomiting without an obvious cause. This can occur alongside other stroke symptoms.

Observe for sudden dizziness, loss of balance, or difficulty walking. These symptoms may appear suddenly during a stroke.

If any one of these symptoms is present, call emergency services immediately. Time is critical. Every minute of delay can increase brain damage.
